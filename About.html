About

SitStayCreate is an example of why we can't have nice things. Trans owned and operated, this website was the target of harrassment and cyberbullying for no reason. I literally made free stuff for you to enjoy using and was repaid in the worst ways. I even provided free support for the software I wrote.

While the software I wrote is still available, I am going to be making a few tweaks in the coming weeks/months/as time and my mental health permit. Consider this a hard fork of serialosc. We like puns and wordgames here, so we'll call it a soft spoon of CerealOSC.

No longer SitStayCreate and disillusioned with the electronic music scene (Read as: I finally understand what is meant by the male-gaze and I'm disgusted. This is not to say that men are bad. There are a lot of great cishet men in my life that are wonderful parents who have been here for me. I will not tolerate man-bashing here any more than I tolerate any sort of bullying) I have decided I need to move into a new direction. My target audience is 35+, probably hella gay, and I do not intend to make commercial products. If I do, it is purely accidental.

When I was young, there was the underground music scene. It wasn't exactly anything goes, but it was able to cover a wider range of topics than the modern lowest-common-denominator must-be-marketable-internationally immediately-available-for-everyone music scenes of today. Not everyone wants to be famous and most of us probably shouldn't be given that level of attention. *Raises both hands in surrender*.

This used to exist in boutique record stores and later on blogs run by "taste makers." Anecdotaly, these were mostly trust-fund adolescents/young adults who had the privilege to spend their time picking who was and wasn't cool. Nowadays we have industry plants labeled as "influencers" that create content for the mostly consolidated market of internet platforms. I don't care really and am not here to change the world, I'm here to reclaim a space for those who aren't comfortable in the spotlight, with an emphasis on safety.

I am McGnars herself and this is the Magic Ohno Show. Welcome to the free-k show. Where non-neurotypicals are celebrated, where no one needs to feel discriminated against, and where everyone is welcome, but most will be confused. My genre is fixed-edge (like straight edge, but we broke it and glued it back together).

Longevity is the name of the game here, not as in having long careers, but as in I need a creative outlet and will focus on maintaining that as a priority over what's fashionable. Some of you will be disappointed by the low-quality, but many of you don't remember DIY or LoFi during their hey-days (I was a tween and pimply teen at the tail-end of these genres). This is a project like Built to Spill where I will put on my friends, but do not promise a constant line-up. The music is probably going to be worse than you expect, but that's all part of the show. The sonic equivalent of backyard wrestling without the arthritis and herniated disks.

Since I like the written word and think there isn't enough of plain-talking new-sincerity outlets and because I know many people who are smarter than I am, I hope to also maintain a diy FreeZine. It will not be released periodically, but instead as time permits and relevant topics present themselves. This is a place for healing, an oasis in the otherwise polluted and overly-politicized web full of hot-takes.
